Internet of Things: Definition

Definition of Internet of Things

The world as we know it is changing day by day. There was a time when all this technology was nothing but a theory. People would dream of making machines that did things for them. However, even in this new era, technology is still growing. Everyday there are new inventions on different technology. Technology has played a big role in making this change happen. Some benefits of technology include:

  • Ease of access to information - The World Wide Web, or www has made the world a global village. Information can be shared from and to any part of the world
  • It saves time - If you want to find out about anything, just go to the internet and in an instant, you'll have all the information you want.
  • Ease of communication - If you want to call that relative of yours in another continent, applications like WhatsApp have been made to make this easier.
  • Innovation in many fields - Technology has led to the digitisation and modernisation of many fields, for example the field of medicine, farming and even electronics. It has made the manufacturing of things easier.
  • Better learning techniques - People are able to learn on the go, from any institution in any part of the world.
These are just but a few benefits of technology.

So what is the Internet of Things?

The Internet of Things(IoT) is the connection of millions of smart devices and sensors connected to the internet. These devices and sensors collect and share data for use and evaluation by many organisations like businesses, cities, governments, hospitals and even individuals. IoT has become one of the most important technologies of the 21st century. Now that we can connect everyday objects like kitchen appliances, cars, thermostats and baby monitors to the internet via embedded devices, seamless communication is possible between people, processes and things.
